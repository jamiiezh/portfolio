Project Overview

The objective of this project is to develop a better understanding of game design and realize the importance of certain level of player interactions as well as challenge level in order to maintain a balance game. In addition, the skills of conducting playtesting sessions including player surveys and player observations are learned to further improve the game.

BRAINSTORM

After playing the original version of the game - Up the River as a team, my group members and I started reflecting the reasons why the game was not fun and wrote in sticky notes the elements the game is lack of.

FIRST ITERATION

In the first iteration, I suggested keeping the original layout of the original version and added currency into the game. The game will become more dynamic by adding more objectives. There will also be freedom for the player to use their skills and decision-making abilities, but will be counteracted by the use of rewards and punishments. The game can also be more people-fun by bringing collaboration between players.

CORE GAMEPLAY

Players move their boats forward towards the harbour through dice roll while staying away from the heavy current of the waterfall, pulling them far from the harbour. In addition, the usage of the good wind/ill wind option, starting over from falling into a harbour trap, winning currency and using it to buy boats, and making a quick, final decision at every turn is introduced.

  • Premise
  • Players are boat owners that are sailing their boats to the harbour, protecting their own boats from falling over the waterfall and at the same time, buying others’ boats in order to be the richest player in the game that owns the most boats.
  • Objective
  • The goal of the game is to keep as many boats as possible by protecting your own boat and buying other opponents’ boats.

FIRST ITERATION REFLECTION

By redesigning the game, we first thought about what was the good thing about the original game that makes it different from other games, and what did not work. Based on my reflection of the game, I realized the original version does not have enough obstacles to be challenging, so I suggested to make the game more challenging in a way to keep player engaged. We then combine our ideas and add more obstacles to it, at the same time keeping the game as simple as possible. In short, we are keep the good thing of the game, and improving the elements that did not work well. The redesign version of the game works well on engaging the players more throughout the game compared to the original one. What did not work well is that the game gets more complicated than the original one, we plan on keeping the rules as simple as possible in the future by having less element changed from the original one.

SECOND ITERATION

After receiving feedback from both the instructors and the playtesters, we started the second iteration. In the second iteration, Li suggested changing the game board to a circular board for a more continuous player experience. I redesigned the game elements and crafted the player pieces as well as the game board to improve its aesthetic.

    IMPROVEMENT

  • I. The revised game is resolved by strategy rather than chance.
  • II. Every player has equal chance to win.
  • III. The game has social interactions, including casual chat and making friends.
  • IV. The game could be played with family or friends as past-time activity to relax.
  • V. The game provide a great forum for players to compete with one another.

PLAYTESTER FEEDBACK

I generated questions that I want to ask playtesters after they playtesting our game with my team. Josh, one of the team members wrote the playtesting script and asks playtesters while I took notes for their answers.

SECOND ITERATION REFLECTION

The biggest change of this iteration is the layout of the game. We changed the line layout to a circle layout. It solves the problem that players always forget to move the end bar to the top after everyone’s move. The new layout of the game also got lots of positive feedback. We also make the game less complicated by changes some rules and layout of the bars.

FINAL ITERATION

With the feedback we received from playtesters and instructors, we started the third iteration. I redesigned the aesthetic of the game board as well as the harbour board using Adobe Illustrator.

REFINE RULE SHEET

During the last two playtesting session, I realized if the game rules are too long, the fun of the game would decrease while reading the rules. So I re-designed the rule sheet with Li using Adobe Illustrator.

QUANTITATIVE DATA TO COLLECT

I generated questions that I want to know from the playtesting session in order to get a clear mind of which part of the game works and which part needs to further improve. We combined the questions together and wrote down the key questions. These questions are more specific compared to the second iteration, and focus on the game elements we have changed for this iteration.

QUANTITATIVE DATA TO COLLECT

After the playtesting session, we gave player survey to the playtesters and generated results to reflect what makes a better game and for future improvement of this game.

Project Evaluation

From this project, I realized the importance of playtesting. Game designer could not only observe how people learn to play their game in a way to reflect themselves, but also hear how other people think about the game. By conducting playtesting session, I learned that making a good rule sheet is critical as it is the first step that people learn about the game, a short and clear rule sheet could boost the interest of people. In addition, a good game does not come from only one iteration, several iterations are needed to get feedback and refine the game.
